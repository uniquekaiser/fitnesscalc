Formulas

Following you will find the formulas used to calculate the fitness and health indicators. Please note not all are based on the same unit system.
BMI Formula:
BMI=W[kg]/(H[m]^2)
Ideal Weight Formulas:
Devine Formula
Men: W[lb] = 110 + 5.06*(H[in]-60)
Women: W[lb] = 100.1 + 5.06*(H[in]-60)
Robinson Formula
Men: W[lb] = 114.4 + 4.18*(H[in]-60)
Women: W[lb] = 107.8 + 3.74*(H[in]-60)
Miller Formula
Men: W[lb] = 123.64 + 3.10*(H[in]-60)
Women: W[lb] = 116.82 + 2.99*(H[in]-60)
The Mufflin equation for RMR:
Men: (10 x w[kg]) + (6.25 x h[cm]) - (5 x Age) + 5
Women: (10 x w[kg]) + (6.25 x h[cm]) - (5 x Age) - 161
The Harris-Benedict equation for BMR:
Men: (13.7516 x w[kg]) + (5.0033 x h[cm]) - (6.755 x Age) + 66.4730
Women: (9.5634 x w[kg]) + (1.8496 x h[cm]) - (4.6756 x Age) + 655.0955
The YMCA Body Fat % formula:
Men: 100*(-98.42 + 4.15*waist[in] - 0.082*w[lb])/w[lb]
Women: 100*(-76.76 + 4.15*waist[in] - 0.082*w[lb])/w[lb]
Jackson, Andrew S. Maximum Heart Rate -Not used-:
HRmax = 206.9 - (0.67 x Age)
Miller et al. Maximum Heart Rate:
HRmax = 217 - (0.85 × Age)
Target Heart Rate (Karvonen method)
THR = ((HRmax − HRrest) × % intensity) + Hrrest

About

Fitness Calculator has the following outputs to help you evaluate your fitness.
BMI – Body Mass Index
BMI is used to find out if a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
The BMI calculation is accuarte for most people, but if you are muscular, or athletic, it might overestimate body fat and report a higher BMI. For low muscle mass it will underestimate body fat and report a lower BMI. WHtR is better for these cases.
Waist-to-Height Ratio
Body shape (determined by Waist-to-Height Ratio) is often considered to be the best indicator of health related to weight.
BMR – Basal Metabolic Rate- and RMR -Resting Metabolic Rate-
BMR stands for Basal Metabolic Rate, and is synonymous with Basal Energy Expenditure or BEE. BMR measurements are typically taken in a darkened room upon waking after 8 hours of sleep; 12 hours of fasting to ensure that the digestive system is inactive; and with the subject resting in a reclining position.

RMR stands for Resting Metabolic Rate, and is synonymous with Resting Energy Expenditure or REE. RMR measurements are typically taken under less restricted conditions than BMR, and do not require that the subject spend the night sleeping in the test facility prior to testing.
Ref: http://www.caloriesperhour.com/tutorial_BMR.php
The App uses the Harris-Benedict (BMR) and The Mufflin (RMR) equations.
Healthy Body Weight Range
The range with the least risk of weight-related health problems. According to BMI (CDC Ranges) and other formulas (Devine, Robinson, Miller).
Daily Caloric Intake
The recommended number of calories from food/drinks to meet activity level. Based on RMR and BMR multiplied by an activity factor (McArdle et al 1996). Then taking into account you weight goal, it will calculate ±500 and give you the recommended lose/gain daily calorie intake.
Personal Target Heart Rate
The heart rate range to achieve particular exercise needs. For this we use the Miller HRmax formula and the Karvonen method.
Body Fat Percentage
Will give you an estimate, using the YMCA formula, of the body fat percentage, which is the total weight of fat divided by total weight; body fat includes essential body fat and storage body fat.
The information displayed on this App is intended to be used as a reference ONLY. Please consult you health specialist before undertaking any weight control plan.
